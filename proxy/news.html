Migration to the openrailwaymap.app domain
June 2025

The public OpenRailwayMap is migrating from the openrailwaymap.fly.dev domain to openrailwaymap.app. Usage of the openrailwaymap.fly.dev domain will redirect to openrailwaymap.app.

Vehicle types for stations
June 2025

Railway stations tagged with a vehicle type, like train=yes, light_rail=yes, subway=yes, tram=yes, monorail=yes or funicular=yes are used to determine the station type when no station = ... is tagged.

Railway museums
June 2025

Railway stations tagged with tourism=museum and museum=railway are shown on the map.

Station and subway entrances
June 2025

Station and subway entrances are shown on the map, and included in the bubble to indicate the (grouped) station area. Entrances are only included when they are part of the public_transport = stop_area relation.

Mobile UI
June 2025

The mobile UI has been improved to reduce the space taken by the buttons and menus. The layer selection has been changed to a popup, and the buttons do not show text anymore for small screen sizes.

Belgian signals
June 2025

Belgian signals are expanded to show aspects of the main signals, render different icons based on the regime, improve speed limit rendering and render station announcements, balise markers, end of platform, stop markers, buffer stops and contact line segmentation.

Catenary masts and portals
June 2025

Catenary masts and portals are shown on the map. Catenary masts with a transition point are shown with a larger icon.

Show station state
May 2025

Stations show their state on the map. The tags construction:railway, proposed:railway, disused:railway, abandoned:railway and preserved:railway are used to show stations under construction, proposed, disused, abandoned or preserved. Razed stations are not shown on the map. Future stations (proposed and under construction) are shown as with a dashed outline (small triangle when zoomed out), while past stations (disused, abandoned and preserved) are shown as a gray circle (small cross when zoomed out).

Polish half-speed signals
May 2025

Half-speed signals for Polish speed signals have been implemented.

May 2025

The popup for railway signals shows all signal tags. This works for recognised signals, but also for unknown signals. Details of signals shown in another layer are also shown (for example main signal details in the speed layer).

Czech Republic signalling

OpenRailwayMap on ARM64
May 2025

The OpenRailwayMap can be deployed on AMD64 and ARM64 hardware. All tests are run on both AMD64 and ARM64 to ensure both architectured keep working as expected.

Edit OpenStreetMap using JOSM
May 2025

The OpenStreetMap editor can be chosen in the configuration screen. It will be used with the general edit button, and when editing a single feature. The default is still iD but JOSM can be chosen as well for more advanced editing capabilities. Editing an OpenHistoricalMap feature will always use iD.

Signal ordering
May 2025

The ordering of signals is taken into account when multiple signals are present on a single feature. This is useful to match the real look of a signal, especially in Germany and Poland where often multiple signals are stacked together.

April 2025
